Edna St. Vincent Millay was a renowned poet and one of the most respected writers of sonnets during the twentieth century. She adopted the traditional form of the sonnet to express progressive and modernist ideas. Her works were numerous including the Pulitzer Prize-winning The Ballad of the Harp-Weaver. She passed away tragically at the age of 58, but her poetry lives on, continuing to touch the lives of countless readers.
